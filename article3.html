The Rise of ESG Reporting

← Back to Articles
August 28, 2025 • Written by Hans • Ciomas, Indonesia • 6 min read

ESG reporting provides a holistic view of a company's impact and sustainability efforts.

Beyond the Bottom Line

For generations, the success of a company was measured almost exclusively by its financial performance—profit, revenue, and market share. Today, that narrow view is rapidly expanding. A global shift in consciousness has led investors, consumers, and regulators to demand greater accountability from corporations regarding their impact on the planet and society. This has given rise to Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting, a new frontier for corporate transparency where accountants play a pivotal role.

Deconstructing ESG

ESG reporting provides a holistic framework for evaluating a company's long-term sustainability and ethical impact. It moves beyond traditional financial metrics to assess performance across three key pillars:

  • Environmental: This pillar scrutinizes a company's environmental footprint. It includes metrics such as carbon emissions, energy efficiency, water consumption, waste management practices, and efforts to protect biodiversity. It answers the question: How does the company steward the natural resources it depends on?
  • Social: The social aspect pertains to how a company manages relationships with its stakeholders—employees, suppliers, customers, and the communities where it operates. It covers critical areas like labor practices, diversity and inclusion policies, employee health and safety, data privacy, and customer satisfaction. It asks: How does the company create value for its people and society?
  • Governance: This deals with a company's internal systems of practice, control, and procedure. It covers topics such as corporate leadership, board composition, executive compensation, audit committee structure, internal controls, and shareholder rights. It addresses: How is the company led and managed for long-term, ethical success?

The Accountant's Crucial Role in a New Paradigm

While many ESG metrics are non-financial in nature, the core skills of an accountant are perfectly suited to manage them. The accounting profession brings an indispensable toolkit of expertise in data collection, verification, assurance, and standardized reporting. Accountants are instrumental in developing robust frameworks and standards for ESG to ensure that the information disclosed is accurate, reliable, and comparable across different companies and industries. Just as they provide assurance on financial statements, accountants are now being called upon to audit and verify ESG data, adding a new layer of trust and credibility to sustainability claims. This evolution solidifies the accountant's position not just as a guardian of financial capital, but as a steward of sustainable value creation.
