Navigating ESG Reporting Standards

A Guide for Modern Accountants

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting has moved from a niche interest to a mainstream requirement for businesses worldwide. Investors, regulators, and consumers are demanding greater transparency on corporate sustainability and ethical practices. This places the accountant at the center of a new and complex reporting landscape.

Accountants are now tasked with collecting, measuring, and assuring a wide range of non-financial data, from carbon emissions to diversity metrics. This requires developing new skill sets and frameworks to ensure that ESG disclosures are as reliable and accurate as traditional financial statements.

Key Challenges and Opportunities

Data Integrity

Ensuring the accuracy and completeness of non-financial data from various sources across the organization.

Standardization

Navigating the complex and evolving landscape of different ESG reporting frameworks and standards.

Assurance and Verification

Providing independent assurance over ESG reports to build trust and credibility with stakeholders.
