The Rise of ESG Reporting

← Back to Articles

August 28, 2025 Â· 6 min read

Beyond the Bottom Line

For generations, the success of a company was measured almost exclusively by its financial performance. Today, that view is rapidly expanding. Investors, consumers, and regulators are increasingly demanding that companies report on their Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) performance. This marks a new frontier for financial reporting, and accountants are at the heart of this transformation.

What is ESG?

ESG reporting provides a holistic view of a company's impact and sustainability. It covers a wide range of non-financial metrics:

  • Environmental: This includes a company's carbon footprint, energy consumption, water usage, waste management, and impact on biodiversity.
  • Social: This pertains to how a company manages relationships with its employees, suppliers, customers, and the communities where it operates. It covers labor practices, diversity and inclusion, data privacy, and customer satisfaction.
  • Governance: This deals with a company's leadership, executive pay, audits, internal controls, and shareholder rights.

The Accountant's Crucial Role

While ESG metrics are non-financial, the skills of an accountant are perfectly suited to manage them. Accountants bring expertise in data collection, verification, assurance, and standardized reporting. The profession is playing a vital role in developing frameworks and standards for ESG reporting to ensure that the information is accurate, reliable, and comparable across companies. Just as accountants provide assurance on financial statements, they are now being called upon to provide assurance on ESG data, adding a new layer of trust and credibility to sustainability claims.
